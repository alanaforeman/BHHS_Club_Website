The E-Nable Club

The eNable Club designs and 3D prints assistive devices for children with motor disabilities, such as cerebral palsy. Through Onshape, an online CAD system, students in this club work collaboratively to brainstorm, compose, and then build prototypes, making continuous adjustments accordingly. Additionally, certain projects may entail using the laser cutter, such as the keyboard cover we designed in 2020. While in the past we have worked collaboratively with Blythedale Children's Hospital, this year, we plan to take on projects from Makers Making Change, whose goal is to achieve economic and social inclusiveness for all people with challenges by connecting makers to those who need assistive devices.
